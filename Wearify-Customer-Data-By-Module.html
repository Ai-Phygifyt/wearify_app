Module 1 of 6
Customer Mobile App  (/c)
Used by the end customer — on their own phone, at home.
Collects
  • ·Phone + OTP
  • ·Name, DOB, gender, city, email
  • ·Height (cm or ft/in)
  • ·Profile photo
  • ·Preferred occasions, fabrics, colours, budget
Sees & Uses
  • ·Their own profile
  • ·Their visit history across stores
  • ·Their try-on looks (AI images)
  • ·Their wishlist & wardrobe
  • ·Their loyalty points & tier
Sends Out
  • ·Nothing to third parties
  • ·All data goes only to the Wearify backend
Key fact for lawyer: this is the customer's own account — but there is currently no "delete my account" button.

Module 2 of 6  ·  Most data-sensitive
Kiosk — the Smart Mirror
Used by the customer in-store, in front of the AI mirror.
Collects
  • ·Phone + OTP (or tablet code)
  • ·Name + DOB (if new customer)
  • ·BODY-SCAN PHOTOGRAPH
  • ·Consent flags (4 of them)
  • ·Feedback rating & comment
Sees & Uses
  • ·Customer's previous looks
  • ·Customer's wardrobe
  • ·Store's saree catalog
  • ·Staff PIN (for sign-in)
  • ·Trial-room code from tablet
Sends Out
  • ·Body scan + saree photo → RunPod GPU (US)
  • ·Order → store dashboard
  • ·Looks stored in Convex
Most important slide for the lawyer. The Kiosk is where the body-scan photo is captured and sent to a US-based GPU service for the AI try-on. This is the platform's most sensitive data flow.

Module 3 of 6
Sales Tablet
Carried by in-store staff while assisting customers.
Collects
  • ·Customer phone (to look up or register)
  • ·Name, DOB, language (if new)
  • ·Occasion & budget for the visit
  • ·Staff PIN (4-digit)
Sees & Uses
  • ·Customer name, segment, visit count
  • ·Customer's previous shortlist
  • ·Store saree catalog
  • ·Active staff sessions
Sends Out
  • ·Trial-room code → customer (to enter on kiosk)
  • ·Shortlist → kiosk session
  • ·Tailor referral with customer name + phone → tailor
Key fact for lawyer: staff can look up any customer by phone at the store they belong to. This is the in-store equivalent of a CRM record.

Module 4 of 6
Store Dashboard
The store owner's web dashboard.
Collects
  • ·Store profile updates only
  • ·Staff records (name, PIN, role)
  • ·Saree catalog edits
  • ·Marketing campaign content
Sees & Uses
  • ·List of customers linked to the store

Module 5 of 6  ·  External partner
Tailor Portal
An independent stitching partner's account.
Collects
  • ·Tailor's own profile + KYC (Aadhaar, PAN, address proof)
  • ·Services & pricing
  • ·Order updates & quotes
  • ·Tailor's own consent flags
Receives Customer Data
  • ·CUSTOMER NAME
  • ·CUSTOMER PHONE
  • ·BODY MEASUREMENTS (bust, waist, etc.)
  • ·Saree, fabric, occasion, budget
  • ·Order status & due date
Sends Out
  • ·Quotes back to customer (via Wearify)
  • ·Tailor may message customer via WhatsApp (out-of-platform)
Important for the lawyer. When a referral becomes a tailor order, the customer's name, phone and body measurements are visible to the tailor — an external partner. There is currently no explicit "share with tailor" consent step.

Module 6 of 6
Admin — Mission Control
Wearify's own operations team.
Collects
  • ·Store onboarding details (store owner KYC)
  • ·Legal documents (privacy notice, terms, DPAs)
  • ·Retention policies (currently defined, not enforced)
  • ·Platform feature flags & config
Sees & Uses
  • ·System-wide metrics (stores, devices, AI agents)
  • ·Health, revenue, ticket views
  • ·Can drill into individual store data
  • ·Reviews tailor KYC documents
Sends Out
  • ·Doesn't directly send customer data externally
  • ·Issues kiosk pairing codes to stores
Key fact for lawyer: Admin has the broadest view across the platform. Every customer record is reachable from here. Today there is no audit log recording what an admin views or does.

Where Data Leaves the Platform
Third Parties That Receive Customer Data
RunPod
United States · GPU
From the Kiosk module
Receives the customer's body-scan photograph + the saree photo (base64), runs the AI try-on, returns the result. No name, phone or ID is sent — only images.
SendGrid
United States · Email
From the Store module
Receives customer email + name + the campaign message. Only triggered when a store sends a campaign.
Twilio
United States · SMS / WhatsApp
From the Store module
Receives customer phone number + the message body. Used for OTP, SMS campaigns and WhatsApp campaigns.
Tailor partners
Independent humans
From the Tablet / Tailor modules
Receive customer name, phone and body measurements when a tailor order is placed. An external human recipient, not a SaaS.

In Closing
What the Lawyer Should Know First
1
The body scan is the most sensitive data we hold
Captured at the Kiosk, stored on the customer record, sent to RunPod (US) for the AI try-on. We have no auto-delete or retention policy on it today.
2
Tailor partners see customer name, phone and measurements
When a customer's order is referred to a tailor, that information becomes visible in the Tailor Portal. No explicit "share with tailor" consent step exists today.
3
There is no "delete my account" anywhere
A customer cannot self-delete their data from the Customer Mobile App, the Kiosk, or anywhere else. Right to erasure (DPDP §12) is not implemented yet.
4
Our age gate is 13+, but DPDP §9 wants 18+ (or verified parental consent)
Customers aged 13–17 are accepted today with no parental-consent step. Counsel to advise on whether to raise the gate to 18 or build a parental-consent flow.
5
Customer data goes to US-based services (RunPod, SendGrid, Twilio)
Data-processing agreements need to be confirmed. The planned Azure-Central-India migration brings primary data residency back inside India.
